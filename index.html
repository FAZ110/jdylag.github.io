The best TV shows

Jan Dyląg

Game of Thrones

Game of Thrones is an epic fantasy television series based on George R.R. Martin's book series "A Song of Ice and Fire." Created by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss, the show became one of the most popular and influential series of all time. Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, Game of Thrones follows the power struggles among noble families as they vie for control of the Iron Throne and, ultimately, rule over the Seven Kingdoms.

The series is known for its intricate and unpredictable storyline, where no character is safe from betrayal or death. It features a large ensemble cast, with complex characters such as Jon Snow, Daenerys Targaryen, Tyrion Lannister, and Arya Stark. Throughout the story, themes of loyalty, honor, and ambition are explored as characters form and break alliances, face moral dilemmas, and confront supernatural forces like White Walkers, who threaten to destroy humanity.

Game of Thrones captivated audiences worldwide with its dramatic storytelling, rich world-building, and high production values. It received numerous awards and critical acclaim for its groundbreaking visuals, complex characters, and mature themes. Although the series' final season sparked significant controversy among fans, Game of Thrones remains a cultural phenomenon that has inspired countless adaptations, discussions, and spin-offs, including the prequel series House of the Dragon.

DEXTER

Dexter is an American crime thriller television series created by James Manos Jr., based on Jeff Lindsay's novel "Darkly Dreaming Dexter." The main character, Dexter Morgan (played by Michael C. Hall), is a seemingly ordinary blood-spatter analyst working for the Miami Police Department. However, Dexter hides a dark secret: he is also a serial killer who targets other criminals who have evaded justice.

Despite his dark urges, Dexter tries to lead a normal life, building relationships and maintaining friendships, but hiding his "dark passenger" becomes increasingly challenging. Over eight seasons, viewers follow his struggles to keep his double life a secret and his inner moral conflicts, which often put his strict personal code to the test.

Dexter gained widespread popularity and critical acclaim for its unique storyline, intense suspense, and the complexity of its main character. The series was praised for Michael C. Hall's outstanding performance and its unconventional exploration of morality and evil. The show also inspired a revival series, Dexter: New Blood, which explores Dexter’s life many years after the events of the original series.
